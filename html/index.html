Home

I am a Humboldt fellow (postdoc) at the Interdisciplinary Center for Scientific Computing (IWR) at the University of Heidelberg working in the Theoretical and Computational Chemistry Group of Prof. Andreas Dreuw. My current research topic is the computational description of phosphorescent iridium complexes as a potential technology for new light sources and displays. Further research interests include the effects of stacking interactions on the excited states of DNA nucleobases and the dynamic description of the resulting phenomena.

Read the "motivation for research" page to get a general overview of why we are doing this kind of work. A more detailed description of my research is provided to explain my specific work and give a short overview over my publications. Feel free to contact me if you have any questions.
